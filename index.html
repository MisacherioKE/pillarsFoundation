Who We Are

Introduction

Pillars Care Foundation is an independent registered non-governmental, non-political, non-religious and non-profit civil society based In Migori – Kenya East Africa. Its prime motto (Aim) is to promote sustainable agribusiness among safeguarding the environment and promoting sustainable development among the disadvantaged common forks Our activities range from Capacity Building to Service Delivery, with focus remaining on improving the current status of Agriculture, Environment and sustainable development through incubating youths and women groups within the society. Whereas in terms of operations what characterise the Organizational functions are its effort to;
a) Enlisting involvement of the local young people and women when initiating the activities at the grass root level.
b) Conserving the natural resources and maintaining the proper hygiene.

About Us

Our history

Together, we have a combined history of over 3 years’ experience in advocacy, lobbying, membership growth, support and capacity building, and working with sector partners. By merging, we are able to offer a range of services for members, government and the wider sector. A key strength of Pillars Care is that we work with and support a diverse membership base across the whole of the Kenya, working across both urban and rural settings. Our members operate across a wide spectrum, offering services including Green Care, community and social based activities. There is often considerable crossover in delivery different settings. For example, a community farm may be involved in both community development and care farming activities. By focusing on the services or activities delivered or facilitated by our members, rather than the physical form upon which our members operate (e.g. a farm, garden, school, orchard), we aim to widen the opportunities open to our members and the wider sector.

Improving People's Lives

Many people assume that community gardens and city or care farms are just nice places to visit where plants are grown and animals kept. But that is far from the whole picture. Many of them also offer an amazing array of benefits and opportunities, which can include education programs, play schemes, healthy living initiatives, work and skills training, social enterprises, volunteer opportunities, environmental schemes, horticultural therapy groups, facilities for people with disabilities…the list goes on and on!

Our work helps:

• 200 villages and school farms
• 1,000 community gardens
• All the Kenya - migori care farms
• Hundreds of farms and gardens in development
Between them our members engage and empower thousands of volunteers and attract over 5000 visitors each year. Their estimated combined annual turnover is in excess of 10,000 visitors
